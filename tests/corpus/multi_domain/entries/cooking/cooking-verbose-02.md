Spent the morning perfecting my sourdough bread technique. I've been maintaining my starter "Bubbles" for 6 months now, feeding it twice daily, and I think I finally cracked the code on getting that perfect ear and open crumb.

The night before, I did an autolyse: mixed 450g bread flour, 50g whole wheat flour, and 375g water. Let it sit covered for 2 hours at room temperature to develop gluten. Then I added 100g of active, bubbly starter and 10g of fine sea salt. Mixed by hand using the pinching method - squeezing the dough between thumb and fingers repeatedly for about 5 minutes.

The bulk fermentation took 5 hours at around 78째F (kept the dough in the oven with just the light on for warmth). Did 4 sets of stretch and folds during the first 2 hours - every 30 minutes. By the end of bulk, the dough had doubled and was puffy with visible bubbles on the surface and sides.

Pre-shaped into a round, let it rest 20 minutes, then did the final shape - tight boule, pulling the surface taut but trying not to degas too much. Placed seam-side up into a rice-flour-dusted banneton, then into the fridge for overnight retard - about 12 hours.

The result: beautiful ear where I scored, crackling crust that sang as it cooled, and when I cut into it - the most gorgeous open crumb I've ever achieved. Large, irregular holes, slightly glossy interior, mild tangy flavor.
